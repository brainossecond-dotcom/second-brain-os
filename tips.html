Study

Study smarter,
not harder.

Techniques that actually work — backed by how your brain really learns.

01

Review your notes within 24 hours

Memory research shows you forget 70% of new information within a day. A quick 10-minute review dramatically improves what you keep.

Study habit
02

Use the 25-5 work rhythm

Work for 25 minutes with full focus, then take a 5-minute break. Your brain needs recovery time to consolidate what you've learned.

Focus technique
03

Build a linked notes system, not a folder dump

Folders isolate your notes. Linked databases (like in Notion) let ideas connect across modules — which is how your brain actually stores information.

Notion tip
04

Teach what you're learning out loud

Explaining a concept aloud — even to yourself — forces you to identify gaps in your understanding. This "protégé effect" is one of the most powerful study methods.

Memory technique
05

Plan your week in Notion every Sunday

A 15-minute weekly review — what's due, what to prioritise, and what to let go — prevents the mid-week panic spiral that kills productivity.

Weekly habit
06

Make your Notion beautiful on purpose

This sounds frivolous — it isn't. When your workspace is aesthetically pleasing, you're more likely to open it, use it, and maintain it. Beauty is functional.

Notion tip
Money

Budget tips that
actually stick.

Simple, honest money advice for students living on real budgets.

01

Track every penny for one month — just once

You don't have to track forever. But doing it once reveals your real spending patterns — and that awareness changes everything, permanently.

Money habit
02

Automate your savings, even if it's £5

Consistent small savings beat occasional large ones. Set a standing order the day your student loan drops — before you have a chance to spend it.

Money habit
03

Use a "wishlist" column before buying anything

Add items to your Notion wishlist with the date you added them. If you still want it 30 days later, then buy it. Impulse purchases vanish under this rule.

Notion tip
04

Scholarships are free money — apply seriously

Most students don't apply because it "takes too long." A 2-hour application for a £500 scholarship is £250/hour. Use our Scholarship Hunter Kit to stay organised.

Student finance

The three-bucket student budget rule

Split your income into: Needs (rent, food, transport), Wants (going out, clothes, fun), and Future (savings, course materials, investments in yourself). Even a rough 60/30/10 split is dramatically better than no split at all. Our free Budget Tracker does this automatically.

Mindset

Motivation &
mindset shifts.

Honest reminders for when student life feels like too much.

01

Progress is not always visible in the moment

Studying and building systems feels slow. But every organised note, every tracked assignment, every reviewed page compounds. Trust the process — it's working.

02

Done is better than perfect, especially with notes

An imperfect Notion page you actually use beats a beautifully planned system you never start. Get something down. Refine later.

03

Rest is part of the work

Sleep and recovery aren't rewards you earn after studying — they're part of how learning actually works. Protect your rest as seriously as you protect your deadlines.
